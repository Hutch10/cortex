Mycology to Your Ecology

Principles:
- Healthy systems move like mycelium: many small nodes sharing nutrients, signals, and memory.
- Drift appears when too much load concentrates in one node and the network cannot redistribute.
- Discovery thrives at the edge where exploratory hyphae test uncertain ground.
- Pruning is not loss; it is network hygiene that restores flow and resilience.
- Diversity of pathways increases recovery speed after shocks.
- Maintenance is an act of intelligence, not a pause in growth.

Operational Lens:
Treat each workflow queue as a living branch.
When one branch crowds out the rest, reroute resources and trim stale tasks.
Use weak signals from field discoveries to guide where to expand next.
